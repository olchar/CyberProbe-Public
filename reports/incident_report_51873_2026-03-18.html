On March 17, 2026 at 09:02 UTC, Microsoft Defender for AI Services detected a User Prompt Injection Attack (UPIA) — also known as a jailbreak — targeting the Azure AI Foundry agent chat_agent_vii (Agent ID: asst_nDelHyfVwFQ7Bz3jFvztVnpQ) deployed on the ais-contoso-procurementagent Cognitive Service. The attack originated from IP 168.61.150.239 (Des Moines, Iowa, US) and was executed through the AIProjectClient azsdk-python-ai-agents/1.2.0b5 SDK running on Linux. This is the first agentic jailbreak detected in this environment — previous attacks targeted standard Azure OpenAI model deployments. The attack was detected by Azure Responsible AI Content Filtering (Prompt Shields). Across the full 15-day investigation window, 12 jailbreak attacks were detected across 3 AI resources, with 4 high-severity anonymized IP access alerts indicating potential threat actor reconnaissance.
